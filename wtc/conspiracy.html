FYI, this Nostradamus quote is bogus.  From
http://urbanlegends.about.com/library/weekly/aa091101b.htm :

Unfortunately, Nostradamus died in 1566, so it's rather unlikely he
wrote this passage in 1654. The quatrain is not to be found in his
published oeuvre. It's a hoax.

...

Nostradamus did not write the quatrain now being attributed to
him. (One wonders how a guy who died in 1566 could have written an
item identified as being penned in 1654 anyway.) It originated with a
student at Brock University in Canada in the 1990s, appearing on a web
page essay on Nostradamus. That particular quatrain was offered by the
page's author, Neil Marshall, as a fabricated example to illustrate
how easily an important-sounding prophecy can be crafted through the
use of abstract imagery. He pointed out how the terms he used were so
deliberately vague they could be interpreted to fit any number of
cataclysmic events.

...

In this case, a very few words actually written by Nostradamus have
been creatively enhanced by person(s) unknown. Notice the addition of
a reference to a "third big war" to the passage already
circulating. Pure bunk.
